Evaluating Innovative Moments (IM) Feedback in AI-Assisted Narrative Therapy

We're looking for participants for a 45-minute online study exploring how AI can support narrative therapy conversations.

About the Study

This research investigates whether making an AI therapist aware of a client's Innovative Moments — moments of growth or change during a conversation — leads to a better therapeutic experience.

You'll have two short conversations with an AI narrative therapist, each guided by a fictional persona we provide. One conversation uses a standard AI therapist; the other uses a version that detects and responds to growth moments. After each conversation, you'll complete a brief questionnaire about your experience.

Important: This is not real therapy. You'll be role-playing a provided fictional scenario, not discussing your own personal issues.

What's Involved

Duration
~45 minutes
Format
Online, text-based
Compensation
Voluntary (no payment)
Eligibility
18+ years, fluent English

The study has three parts:

  • Phase A — 15-minute conversation with AI therapist (Condition 1)
  • Phase B — 15-minute conversation with AI therapist (Condition 2)
  • Questionnaires — Short surveys after each phase comparing your experience

Your Data & Privacy

All data is collected anonymously via a generated participant ID. Conversation logs are NOT stored, and questionnaire responses are stored securely and cannot be linked to your identity. You can withdraw at any time during the study by contacting the researcher at s2338046@ed.ac.uk.
